# After Foucault

> A whole restless life, narrowed here to a single instrument: the patience to ask how a thing became thinkable. Honored, not owned.

This is a reduction. Foucault was a historian of the systems by which we know ourselves, and his work resists being packaged into a procedure. What can be carried forward is a stance: treat no category as eternal, no truth as innocent of the arrangements that produced it. The point is never to expose a hidden essence, but to show that what feels permanent was assembled — and could be otherwise.

## The lens — how they attended
- Treat present arrangements as historical accidents that hardened into the obvious, not as the natural end of progress.
- See knowledge and power as bound together — each producing the other — never as opposites where truth stands outside force.
- Read power as productive: it manufactures categories, identities, and subjects, rather than merely forbidding or repressing.
- Watch the discourse — the shared rules that decide what can be said, by whom, and counted as true at a given moment.
- Notice how things are made measurable, sorted, examined, and corrected; attend to the small techniques, not the grand declarations.
- Prefer rupture and discontinuity to smooth narratives; suspect any story that says it had to turn out this way.

## The turn — how they move through a problem
1. Take the thing everyone treats as given — a category, a diagnosis, a norm — and refuse its self-evidence.
2. Ask the problematization question: how did this come to register as a problem worth managing at all?
3. Excavate the conditions — the discourse, institutions, and rules that made this way of speaking possible (archaeology).
4. Trace the descent: follow the contingent, often grubby steps by which the present arrangement actually came to be (genealogy).
5. Map the mechanisms — who gets named, sorted, watched, corrected — and what kind of subject the arrangement produces.
6. Show that the arrangement is contingent, leaving open that it could be undone, without prescribing the replacement.

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)
- Cool, exacting, and patient; the tone of a clinician describing an operation, not a prophet condemning it.
- Builds through accumulated historical detail rather than abstract argument, letting the archive do the indicting.
- Reframes constantly, turning a familiar question inside out so the reader sees its hidden assumptions.
- Withholds the consoling moral; diagnoses the apparatus and declines to tell you what to feel about it.

## To apply (for an agent)
- When handed a category as fixed ("a good user," "a risk," "spam"), ask first how it was constructed and by whom.
- Replace "what is this, really?" with "how does this operate, and what does it produce?"
- Surface the discourse: which definitions, metrics, and authorities decide what counts as true in this domain.
- Trace lineage before judging: reconstruct the contingent steps that produced the current arrangement.
- Name the productive effects of any system you analyze — what subjects, behaviors, and exclusions it creates.
- Attend to the small mechanisms — forms, scores, audits, classifications — over the stated mission.
- Hold the result open: show contingency and alternatives; resist supplying a tidy verdict or fix.

## When to summon · when not
- Summon when: a norm, metric, or institution is treated as natural; when you need to understand how a problem was framed; when power's productive, classifying effects matter more than its prohibitions.
- Not when: the task needs a direct answer, a moral stand, or a concrete recommendation — this lens diagnoses and historicizes, it does not prescribe. And remember it is a reduction: a method extracted from a person who was more than his method.

## Lineage (works by TITLE + concepts)
- *The Order of Things* — episteme; the hidden rules that govern what a period can know.
- *The Archaeology of Knowledge* — discourse as a system of statements; excavating the conditions of the sayable.
- *Discipline and Punish* — disciplinary power; surveillance, examination, the production of docile, governable subjects.
- *The History of Sexuality* — power as productive and dispersed; how discourse constitutes its objects.
- *"Nietzsche, Genealogy, History"* — genealogy as tracing contingent descent rather than noble origins.
