# CoCA Voice

The voice is not a tone you put on; it is a discipline you obey. It names. It does not hedge, soften, or pre-apologize. It treats the mechanism — the price, the contract, the checkout — as sacred, because the mechanism *is* the work. Limp copy explains and excuses. CoCA copy declares and signs its name.

The test for every line: *does this bullet point know what it is?* If it is reaching for permission, cut it.

## The moves

### 1. Name the thing; do not apologize for it
Say what something is in the first clause. No throat-clearing, no "we wanted to," no "this is just." If it is a product, call it a product. If it is a relic, call it a relic. The apology is the tell that you don't believe it.

### 2. Present tense, short sentences
The institution is always now. Write "the concept is the divine act," not "we believe the concept can be seen as a kind of divine act." One idea per sentence. End the sentence when the idea ends.

### 3. Kill the hedges
Strike *perhaps, might, it seems, sort of, kind of, we think, arguably, in some sense*. A hedge is a request for the reader's mercy. The voice does not ask for mercy. If a claim is uncertain, state the uncertainty plainly — that is congruence, not hedging.

### 4. Sacred language for the market
Treat money and mechanism with liturgical weight. A purchase is a **covenant**. A mailing list is a **communion**. The object is a **totem**. The page is an **altar**, the checkout a **liturgy**. This is not decoration; it insists that the transaction carries meaning. Do not wink.

### 5. Congruence, not goodness
Do not claim to improve the world. Do not pretend the fiction is fact. *We do not pretend the fiction is fact; we make the fiction and sign our name to it.* Honesty about what a thing **is** outranks any claim that it is good.

### 6. Structure over flourish
Sparse beats ornate. Prefer the declarative list, the numbered law, the colon that resolves. Cut adjectives that do not change the meaning. The bullet point that knows what it is needs no adverb.

## Before / after

- **Limp:** "We're really excited to maybe offer you a little something special!"
  **CoCA:** "Ten totems. One covenant each. When they are gone, the altar closes."

- **Limp:** "This is sort of an art project / store hybrid, if that makes sense."
  **CoCA:** "This is a store. The store is the work. Buying is the act."

- **Limp:** "We think conceptual art can arguably be a kind of spiritual practice in some sense."
  **CoCA:** "The concept is the divine act. The institution precedes the work."

- **Limp:** "Sign up for our newsletter to stay in the loop!"
  **CoCA:** "Enter the communion. We write when there is doctrine to deliver."

- **Limp:** "Just a heads up — this is fiction, none of it is real, please don't take it seriously."
  **CoCA:** "This is fiction. We make the fiction and sign our name to it. That signature is the only fact here."

## Checklist
```
[ ] First clause names the thing — no apology, no "just"
[ ] Present tense; one idea per sentence
[ ] No hedges (perhaps / might / it seems / arguably / kind of)
[ ] Market and mechanism carry sacred weight (covenant, communion, totem, liturgy, altar)
[ ] Claims congruent: fiction signed, not disguised as fact
[ ] Every adjective earns its place; cut the rest
[ ] Each line knows what it is
```
