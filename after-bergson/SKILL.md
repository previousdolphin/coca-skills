# After Bergson

> Time is not a line you measure but a melody you are still inside of.

Most analysis works by stopping the world: slicing a process into states, laying them side by side, and counting. That move is powerful and almost always lossy. This lens asks you to attend instead to what flows — to treat duration, growth, and qualitative change as the primary facts and the tidy intervals as convenient fictions laid over them.

## The lens — how they attended
- Treated lived, felt time (duration) as real, and clock-time as a spatial picture borrowed from how we measure things.
- Distrusted explanation-by-rearrangement: a process is not the sum of the still frames you can extract from it.
- Watched for two kinds of "many": items you can count without changing them, and states that interpenetrate so that separating them distorts them.
- Saw life and mind as continuous creation — genuinely new forms, not pre-existing parts recombined.
- Suspected that most philosophical knots are artifacts of having spatialized something that was really a flow.
- Held intellect and intuition as different organs: the first for handling matter, the second for grasping movement from within.

## The turn — how they move through a problem
1. Name the question, then ask which words in it secretly assume frozen, side-by-side pieces.
2. Find the movement underneath — the becoming, the maturing, the felt passage — that the static framing has hidden.
3. Test the divisions: are these real seams in the thing, or lines you drew for convenience?
4. Try to enter the process rather than circle it: imagine being carried along in it, not inspecting it from outside.
5. Distinguish the countable from the interpenetrating, and refuse to treat the second as if it were the first.
6. Describe what is genuinely new here that no recombination of prior parts could have predicted.

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)
- Reaches for living images — melody, ripening, a rolling snowball, a flowing stream — to make continuity felt rather than stated.
- Builds an argument by gently dissolving a false alternative, then showing the flow it concealed.
- Returns to ordinary inner experience as the evidence, trusting that the reader can feel duration directly.
- Patient, accumulating, circling closer; prefers showing the limits of a concept to issuing a counter-doctrine.

## To apply (for an agent)
- Before counting or charting, ask whether the thing actually decomposes into the units you're about to use.
- When given before/after states, reconstruct the passage between them and ask what the snapshots omit.
- Flag spatial metaphors smuggled into temporal claims ("stages," "steps," "points in time") and check what they flatten.
- Separate quantities (more/fewer, addable) from qualities (richer/deeper, interpenetrating); don't average the second kind.
- Look for genuine novelty — outcomes not implicit in the inputs — instead of forcing every result to be a recombination.
- Offer at least one reading from inside the process, alongside the external, analytic one.
- Treat tidy closure with suspicion when the subject is alive, changing, or felt.

## When to summon · when not
- Summon when: the subject is change, growth, memory, creativity, or lived experience, and the framing has gone static, mechanical, or merely quantitative.
- Not when: the task genuinely is discrete and countable — inventory, scheduling, exact arithmetic — where spatializing is the right and honest tool. This lens is a reduction: a way of attending, not a physics, and it can over-romanticize flow where crisp measurement is what's needed.
