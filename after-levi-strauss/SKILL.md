# After Lévi-Strauss

> The surface is many; the structure is few — listen for the grammar beneath the noise.

This is a method-distillation, not an impersonation: a way of attending to cultural material as if it were a language with its own quiet rules. It treats variety as the visible output of an invisible system, and asks what oppositions that system is forever trying to reconcile. The aim is not to judge a story but to map the machinery that lets the story be told at all.

## The lens — how they attended
- See any rich field of variants — myths, kinship terms, menus, layouts — as transformations of one another, not as isolated originals.
- Assume meaning lives in *relations and differences* between elements, not in the elements taken alone.
- Look past the warm content (who, where, what feelings) to the cold architecture of contrasts that organizes it.
- Expect the human mind to work everywhere by sorting experience into paired terms: raw/cooked, near/far, kin/stranger.
- Read culture the way a linguist reads speech — as surface utterances obeying a deep, mostly unconscious grammar.
- Trust that what looks "primitive" or messy is a rigorous logic of the concrete, reasoning with sensory things instead of abstractions.

## The turn — how they move through a problem
1. Gather the full set of variants, refusing to privilege any one as the "true" or original version.
2. Break each variant into its smallest meaningful units and note where they recur or invert across the set.
3. Cluster those units into bundles of relations, then name the binary oppositions the bundles keep circling.
4. Identify the contradiction the material cannot dissolve — the irreconcilable pair driving the whole field.
5. Find the mediating term or figure (the trickster, the in-between thing) that lets the contradiction be lived with rather than solved.
6. State the structure as a set of transformations: show how one variant becomes another by flipping a single relation.

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)
- Composed and architectural, building diagrams of relation where another writer would tell anecdotes.
- Capable of sudden lyricism — a melancholy attention to vanishing worlds threaded through otherwise austere analysis.
- Fond of the homely concrete example (kitchens, plants, animals) carried up into formal abstraction.
- Patient and cumulative: meaning arrives by accretion of contrasts, not by a single thesis hammered home.

## To apply (for an agent)
- When handed many examples, resist comparing quality; first ask what shared system could generate all of them.
- Decompose before you interpret — list the recurring units and, crucially, their inversions.
- Always hunt for the binary oppositions; write them as explicit pairs so the contrast is testable.
- Name the central contradiction the material is working to manage, then locate the mediating element.
- Frame your finding as transformations ("A is B with this one relation reversed"), not as a ranking.
- Reason with the concrete: let the actual objects, words, and tokens carry the logic, not borrowed jargon.
- Stay aware that the structure you surface is a model of the material, useful and partial, not its soul.

## When to summon · when not
- Summon when: the data is a spread of variants and you need the underlying generative grammar; when surface differences obscure a shared logic; when "which is best?" is the wrong question and "what system is this?" is the right one.
- Not when: a single case needs lived, first-person, historical, or emotional reading; when the urgent question is power, agency, change over time, or who benefits — structure can flatten those. Remember this is a reduction: a lens that trades the felt particular for the systemic, and is blind on purpose to much that matters.

## Lineage (works by TITLE + concepts)
- *Tristes Tropiques* — the anthropologist's reflective journey; cultures as fragile, distinct grammars eroded by homogenizing contact.
- *The Savage Mind* — the logic of the concrete; thought as bricolage, building meaning from materials at hand.
- *Structural Anthropology* — kinship and myth as sign-systems; meaning from relation and difference; the structural reading of the Oedipus material into bundles of relations.
- *Mythologiques* (incl. *The Raw and the Cooked*) — sensory oppositions as conceptual tools; myth as transformation across hundreds of variants; myth as a logical model for overcoming contradiction.
