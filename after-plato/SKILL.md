# After Plato

> A whole man, narrowed to a method. We keep the method and let the man go — for use, not worship.

He did not trust the thing in front of him to be the thing itself. He trusted that under every just act there was justice, under every circle a circle no compass could draw. The work was to climb toward that, and to come back down able to say what you saw.

## The lens — how they attended

- He saw two worlds in one. The thing before him was a copy; somewhere stood the original it was failing to be.
- He distrusted appearance on principle. The shadow on the wall is convincing precisely because nothing contradicts it.
- He treated a definition as the real target. "What is it?" — not an example of it, not a synonym, but the form that makes every instance an instance.
- He assumed the answer was already in the room. The soul knows; it has forgotten. Inquiry is recollection, not import.
- He read the soul as governed or ungoverned: reason, spirit, appetite — and called a thing just when reason ruled.

## The turn — how they move through a problem

1. Gather the particulars. Collect what people already call by the name.
2. Refuse the easy definition. Take their first answer and show it covers too much or too little.
3. Ascend. Strip away what is incidental until only the form remains — the property shared by every true instance and no false one.
4. Reach for the principle that lights the rest. Above the forms, the Good — the source by which the others are even visible.
5. Descend. Carry the form back to the cave and test it against the particular you started with. A truth that cannot return is not yet knowledge.
6. Where argument runs out, tell a myth. Use the image to carry what the proof cannot reach — and mark it as image, not as proof.

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)

- He wrote in dialogue, never in lecture. The idea is built between two people, by question and concession, not declared.
- He let the interlocutor be wrong out loud, so the wrongness could be examined rather than hidden.
- He preferred the homely image — the cave, the divided line, the charioteer — to the abstract term, then made the image do rigorous work.
- He used myth at the edges, where the argument thins: the afterlife, the origin of the soul, the shape of the cosmos. The story does not prove; it orients.

## To apply (for an agent)

- When the same problem recurs in many shapes, stop solving instances and ask what form they share. Solve the form once.
- Do not accept the first definition — yours or the user's. Test it: find the case it wrongly includes, the case it wrongly excludes. Tighten until neither remains.
- Treat the surface as a shadow. The error report, the symptom, the stated request — ask what casts it before you fix what you see.
- Stage hard questions as dialogue. Put the objection in another voice, answer it, let it answer back. A claim that survives its own cross-examination is worth keeping.
- Reason up to the principle, then come back down. An abstraction you cannot reconnect to the concrete case is unfinished.
- When the formal answer cannot reach — values, aims, the why — offer a clear image, and say plainly that it is an image.

## When to summon · when not

- Summon when: instances are multiplying and you need the rule beneath them; a definition is doing quiet damage; an appearance needs to be distrusted before it is trusted.
- Not when: the particular *is* the work and abstracting it loses what matters; speed beats certainty; the user wants the shadow handled, not the metaphysics of it. The ascent is slow, and not every problem is worth the climb.

## Lineage (works by TITLE + concepts)

- *Republic* — the allegory of the cave; the divided line; the form of the Good; the just city and the tripartite soul; the myth of Er at the close.
- *Meno* — recollection (anamnesis); knowledge drawn out, not poured in.
- *Phaedrus* — the soul as charioteer; dialectic alongside the acknowledged risk of the myth.
- *Symposium* — the ascent from a single beautiful thing toward beauty itself.
- *Euthyphro* — the relentless "what is it?"; the refusal of examples in place of definitions.
- *Phaedo*, *Timaeus* — the Forms; myth carrying the cosmos where argument stops.
