# Pre-Mortem

A post-mortem explains a failure after it happens. A pre-mortem imagines the failure *before you begin* and works backward to its causes. Gary Klein's move exploits **prospective hindsight**: people asked why something *did* fail generate roughly 30% more reasons, and more concrete ones, than people asked what *might* go wrong. Certainty of failure loosens the tongue that optimism keeps polite.

This is **Perpetual Dissolution**: dismantle the plan's confidence before it calcifies. A plan held too tightly stops being a plan and becomes a belief.

## The moves

1. **Frame the failure as already true.** Say it out loud: "It's a year from now. This project shipped and failed — completely. It's a disaster." Not "could fail" — *did* fail. The grammar matters.
2. **Each participant writes silently.** Everyone lists, alone and in writing, every reason it failed. Silent and independent first — no anchoring on the loudest voice, no groupthink. Aim for quantity.
3. **Round-robin to a master list.** Go around the room, one reason each per turn, until every reason is on a shared list. No debating yet — just collect.
4. **Categorize and mitigate.** Cluster the reasons. For each cluster that matters, name a prevention or an early-warning signal.
5. **Fold mitigations into the plan.** A pre-mortem that doesn't change the plan was theater. Edit the plan; assign owners.

## Distinguish it from

- **Post-mortem:** after the fact, explains real failure. Pre-mortem is before, on an imagined one.
- **Ordinary risk list:** "what might go wrong" — hedged, abstract, optimism intact. The pre-mortem's *assumed certainty of failure* is what unlocks the candid, specific reasons.

## Protocol

```
FRAME:      "One year out. This failed — completely. Why?"
SILENT:     each person writes every reason, alone, in writing
COLLECT:    round-robin → one master list (no debate)
CLUSTER:    group reasons into themes
MITIGATE:   per theme → prevention + early-warning signal + owner
FOLD IN:    edit the plan; assign owners; set the warning triggers
```

Use the companion `premortem-template.md` to run the session.

## When to reach for it

- At the start of any significant project, launch, migration, or irreversible commitment — before momentum makes dissent expensive.
- When a team is suspiciously unanimous and confident.

## Failure modes this prevents

- The plan no one would question out loud.
- Risks visible to one quiet person who never got the floor.
- Confident momentum that only meets reality on launch day.
