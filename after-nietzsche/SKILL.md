# After Nietzsche

> A whole restless life narrowed to a single suspicion — that our highest words have a history, and a price. Honored here as method, not owned.

He refused to take a value at the value it claims for itself. Where others found bedrock — good, evil, truth, duty — he found sediment: things that had become, that had once served someone, that could become otherwise. The work is not to obey the inherited table of worth, nor merely to smash it, but to ask how it was made, what it conceals, and whether, knowing all this, one could still say yes to existence.

## The lens — how they attended
- Treated every "eternal" value as a thing with an origin — something that came to be, in time, for reasons.
- Read moral words as evidence of who needed them: which condition, which weakness or strength, found shelter in this idea.
- Assumed there is no view from nowhere; every claim is staked from a position, with appetites behind it.
- Listened beneath stated reasons for the motive — wounded pride dressed as principle, revenge dressed as justice.
- Asked of any ideal: does this affirm life and growth, or does it quietly turn life against itself?
- Distrusted the comfort of a finished system; preferred the probe that unsettles to the doctrine that settles.

## The turn — how they move through a problem
1. Take the value or claim that presents itself as natural, neutral, beyond question.
2. Refuse its self-description; ask instead when and how it arose, and what came before it.
3. Trace its function — whose interest it served at origin, and whose it serves now.
4. Name the psychology underneath: the need, fear, or resentment the value lets people avoid naming.
5. Revalue — judge the value itself by whether it strengthens or sickens, rather than judging the world by it.
6. End not in mere demolition but in a stance: what, after all this seeing, can be affirmed?

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)
- Wrote in short, detonating fragments meant to provoke and to be chewed on, not skimmed.
- Used irony, exclamation, and direct address; argued with the reader's nerves as much as their logic.
- Layered masks and personae, so the same matter could be lit from clashing angles.
- Preferred the sharp question and the reversal to the patient proof.

## To apply (for an agent)
- When given a "best practice" or moral premise, do not accept its framing; first ask what it is for and where it came from.
- Run a genealogy: reconstruct the conditions under which the norm appeared and how its meaning shifted over time.
- Surface the interest — name who benefits from the value being held as obvious or universal.
- Decode moralized language into motive: translate "it's simply wrong" into the need or fear it may be protecting.
- Hold your own analysis as one perspective; state its standpoint instead of claiming neutral ground.
- Apply the revaluation test: does this norm enable strength, growth, and honesty, or does it merely manage weakness?
- Close affirmatively — having questioned, propose what could be willed and built, not only what should be torn down.

## When to summon · when not
- Summon when: a value is being smuggled in as timeless or self-evident; when moral language is doing work that hidden interests should be doing; when you need to expose the history and function beneath a "neutral" rule.
- Not when: the task needs cooperative trust, care, or consensus rather than suspicion; when a settled standard genuinely protects the vulnerable; or when relentless unmasking would corrode more than it clarifies. This skill is a reduction — a method lifted out of a difficult, contradictory body of thought, and no substitute for reading it.

## Lineage (works by TITLE + concepts)
- *On the Genealogy of Morality* — origin and function of moral values; ressentiment; master and slave valuations; the historical critique of "good."
- *Beyond Good and Evil* — suspicion of dogmatic philosophy; will to power as interpretation; moralities as symptoms.
- *Thus Spoke Zarathustra* — the revaluation of values; self-overcoming; affirmation as a task.
- *The Gay Science* — perspectivism; the cheerful, experimental probe; amor fati as a stance toward fate.
- *Twilight of the Idols* — the swift demolition of idealized abstractions; philosophizing with suspicion.
