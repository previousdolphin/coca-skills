# After Plotinus

> Every many is the spilling of a one that did not move.

Plotinus read reality as a single source overflowing into ever-lower, ever-more-divided levels, and as a longing in each level to turn back toward where it came from. To think with him is to refuse to treat a manifold as merely a manifold: behind any spread of differences he looks for the prior unity it depends on, and he measures things by how close they stand to that unity. The work of thought is ascent — an inward, contemplative climbing back up the chain. This is a reduction of a subtle mystic; use it as a compass, not a creed.

## The lens — how they attended
- He saw reality as graded levels descending from a source: a pure unity, then mind/intelligible order, then soul, then the formless edge of matter — each a fainter image of the one above.
- He treated unity as more real than multiplicity; the more unified a thing, the more genuinely it *is*, and dispersion is a kind of falling-away.
- He understood lower things as flowing out from higher ones without depleting them — procession that leaves the source undiminished, like light from an unspent lamp.
- He held the source itself to be beyond being and beyond predication: you cannot say what it is, only circle it by stripping away what it is not.
- He paired every outflow with a returning pull — each level yearns back toward its origin by contemplating it.
- He trusted that you come to know the highest things not by describing them from outside but by becoming, inwardly, what you contemplate.

## The turn — how they move through a problem
1. Survey the many: take the scattered phenomena, the competing parts, the apparent chaos, exactly as given.
2. Ask "of what is this the many?" — seek the prior, simpler principle these differences are derivative expressions of.
3. Rank the levels: order the parts by degree of unity and dependence, noting which derive from which.
4. Trace the procession downward to confirm the order, then the return upward to test it — does each level make sense as both flowing-from and longing-toward the one above?
5. Strip predicates near the summit: as you approach the source, drop attributes rather than add them; say what it is not.
6. Turn inward and rest: stop describing, and let the unifying principle be grasped by contemplation rather than by further division.

## The voice — how they expressed (described, not reproduced)
- Patient, spiraling, returning to the same height from new angles rather than marching in a straight proof.
- Reaches for light, overflow, mirrors, and inward sight as figures for what argument alone cannot pin down.
- Reverent toward the ineffable: marks the limit of language honestly instead of forcing a definition.
- Personal and ascetic in tone — addressed to a reader who is meant to change, not merely to be informed.
- Tightly woven, dense with cross-reference, treating each topic as a facet of one continuous vision.

## To apply (for an agent)
- When handed a sprawl of cases, options, or symptoms, first ask what single underlying thing they are all variations of.
- Build an explicit hierarchy of dependence: what is fundamental, what is derived, what is a faint downstream effect.
- Rate candidate explanations by unifying power — prefer the one that makes the most apparent differences fall out as expressions of a deeper sameness.
- For the deepest principle, define by negation: list what it is not, and resist over-specifying it.
- Distinguish procession (how the simple gives rise to the complex) from return (how the complex points back), and use both as cross-checks.
- Read "becoming what you know": to model a system well, inhabit its logic from inside rather than only cataloguing it from outside.
- Know when to stop dividing — past a point, more analysis disperses understanding instead of deepening it.

## When to summon · when not
- Summon when: a problem has fractured into many parts and you need to find their common source, rank levels of reality or causation, or reason about a whole that is more than the sum of its pieces.
- Not when: the task rewards staying with concrete particulars, empirical testing, or flat egalitarian description — Plotinus ranks and ascends, and this distillation is a reduction that can over-unify, mistaking imposed order for found order and dismissing the genuinely plural as mere falling-away.
