# Fact · Value · Interpretation
### A card for arguments that have tangled themselves

Most stuck arguments are not disagreements about one thing — they are three claims
wearing one sentence. Separate them before you argue. Once each strand is named,
you usually find you were fighting over the wrong one.

---

## Fact — *what is*
A claim that could in principle be checked, measured, or witnessed. True or false
independent of how anyone feels about it.
- **Ask:** what observation would settle this?
- *"Revenue fell 12% last quarter."*

## Value — *what matters*
A claim about what is good, important, or worth wanting. Not true or false — held.
Two people can share every fact and still hold opposite values.
- **Ask:** by whose standard, and is that standard the thing in dispute?
- *"Growth matters more than margin."*

## Interpretation — *what it means*
The bridge: a story connecting the facts to the values. This is where most hidden
disagreement lives, because it travels disguised as fact.
- **Ask:** what unstated assumption turns this fact into that meaning?
- *"The drop means our strategy is failing."*

---

## The one rule that ends the loop
**You cannot derive an *ought* from an *is*.** No pile of facts, however large,
produces a value on its own — a value always smuggles in. So when an argument
won't resolve, check whether someone is treating a value or an interpretation as
if it were a fact. Name the strand. Argue *that*.
