# One-Way & Two-Way Doors
### A decision-speed card

Before you deliberate, classify the decision. The reversible ones do not deserve
the caution you give the irreversible ones — and most decisions are reversible.
Spending one-way-door rigor on a two-way door is how good teams grind to a halt.

---

## One-way doors — *irreversible*
Walking through is costly or impossible to undo: shutting down a product, a public
launch, a hire/fire, a contract you can't exit, a thing you can't unsay.
- **Analyze hard.** Gather the data; seek dissent; sleep on it.
- **Move slow.** The cost of being wrong is high and sticky. Earn high confidence first.

## Two-way doors — *reversible*
You can walk back through and the room looks the same: a trial, a draft, a toggle,
an experiment, a reversible config, most internal choices.
- **Move at ~70% confidence.** Waiting for 90% costs more than the occasional wrong turn.
- **Move fast.** Decide, watch what happens, reverse if it was wrong. Speed *is* the strategy.

---

## The discipline
1. Ask first: **is this a one-way or a two-way door?**
2. Be honest — fear disguises two-way doors as one-way doors.
3. Most decisions are two-way. Treat them that way and you reclaim enormous speed.
4. Reserve slow, exhaustive analysis for the few doors that truly only open once.
