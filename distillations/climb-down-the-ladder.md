# Climb Down the Ladder
### A diagnostic card for the Ladder of Inference

We climb from raw data to action in a fraction of a second, and most of the climb
is invisible. When a conclusion feels obvious — or an argument is stuck — climb
back down. Ask one descending question per rung until you reach the data everyone
can actually see.

---

## 7. Actions — *what I do*
What am I about to do or recommend, and on what authority?

## 6. Beliefs — *what I now hold as true about the world*
What general belief did this harden into — and would I apply it to the next case automatically?

## 5. Conclusions — *what I concluded*
What did I decide here, and would someone with the same data necessarily conclude the same?

## 4. Assumptions — *what I assumed*
What did I take for granted to get from the meaning to the conclusion?

## 3. Added meaning — *what I made it mean*
What story or intent did I read into the data that the data did not itself contain?

## 2. Selected data — *what I noticed*
Of everything available, what did I select — and what did I ignore or never look at?

## 1. Observable data — *what is actually there*
Strip interpretation: what would a camera have recorded? Start the argument here.

---

The reflexive loop: our beliefs shape which data we select next, so the ladder
quietly feeds itself. Climbing down breaks the loop. Argue from rung 1, not rung 7.
