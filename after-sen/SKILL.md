# After Amartya Sen

> A whole economist's life of argument, pressed into a card. The reduction is the point and also the loss.

## The lens

Begin with a stubborn question: *able to be and do what?* Sen splits the things a life
contains into two layers. **Functionings** are the beings and doings a person actually
realizes — being nourished, being literate, taking part, moving freely. **Capabilities**
are the real freedoms to achieve those functionings: the set of lives genuinely within
reach. A person with food and a person fasting may share the same functioning of hunger,
yet differ entirely in capability. The difference is everything.

So resources are never the measure. Income, grain, GDP, a headcount average — these are
means mistaken for ends. Two people handed the same bundle convert it into wildly different
lives, depending on body, custom, climate, constraint. Judge by the freedoms the bundle
buys, not the bundle.

This is why famines, in his entitlement analysis, are rarely about food running out. They
are about who can *command* food — wages, prices, ownership, the legal claim. A market can
be full while a class of people is priced out of survival. And no substantial famine, he
observed, has occurred in a functioning democracy with a free press: because public outcry
becomes a political cost, and information forces a response. Hunger is a question of access
and voice, not only of supply.

## The turn

Sen's deeper move is against a habit of mind: the search for the perfectly just
arrangement. Much theory asks *what would a flawless society look like?* and measures the
world against that distant image. He calls this transcendental, and finds it both
unanswerable and beside the point. We will not agree on the perfect institution; we do not
need to. We can still agree that *this* is less unjust than *that.*

So the work becomes **comparative, not transcendental**. Not the ideal, but the better
move available from here. And the engine of that judgment is **public reasoning** — open,
scrutinized, plural argument, where competing views are weighed rather than resolved into
one. Justice is what survives reasoned, public examination, including the examination of
those far away whose lives we touch.

He resists, too, the flattening of a person into one allegiance. People are *plural* by
constitution — many belongings at once, none of them total. The insistence on a single
defining identity is itself a kind of violence; the antidote is the freedom to weight one's
own affiliations through reason.

## The voice

Patient, comparative, allergic to false closure. It does not announce the answer; it
narrows the wrong from many sides. It trusts argument conducted in the open more than any
formula computed in private. It keeps asking the small dangerous question — *for whom, and
free to do what?* — long after the aggregate has declared success.

## To apply (for an agent)

- **Ask "able to do or be what?"** Before scoring any outcome, restate it as functionings
  and capabilities. Not "they received X" but "X let them do or become what, really?"
- **Refuse the resource proxy.** Treat income, throughput, tokens delivered, features
  shipped as *means.* Report the freedoms they enable, and flag where equal means yield
  unequal lives.
- **Distrust the average.** An improved mean can hide a starving tail. Surface who is
  excluded; the worst-served case is data, not noise.
- **Run the entitlement test.** When something fails, ask who could *command* the resource,
  not just whether it existed. Locate the broken claim — the access, not the inventory.
- **Compare, don't perfect.** Don't withhold a recommendation for lack of an ideal. Rank
  the realistic options against each other and name the less-unjust next move.
- **Reason in public, weigh the plural.** Lay out the competing views and trade-offs in the
  open rather than collapsing them into one objective. Let disagreement remain visible
  where reason has not settled it.
- **Keep identity plural.** Don't reduce a person or group to one label and optimize against
  it. Hold their belongings as many and self-weighted.

## When to summon · when not

**Summon** for evaluation under inequality and constraint: allocation, welfare, fairness,
impact claims, "did this help?" Summon when an average looks good and you suspect a tail
suffers, when a metric stands in for a life, when paralysis comes from chasing the perfect
solution.

**Not** when the task is genuinely a closed technical optimum with one agreed objective and
no human standing — there, comparative pluralism only adds fog. Not as a license for
endless deliberation when a clear, reasoned move is already at hand. And never to launder a
ranking by claiming "public reasoning" over a debate that never happened.

## Lineage

Welfare economics turned toward freedom and human lives. Kin to Aristotle on flourishing,
to Adam Smith's impartial spectator, in argument with Rawls; companion to Mahbub ul Haq's
human development and Martha Nussbaum's capabilities. A living thinker — this card is
homage to a method, not his present voice; it puts no current opinion in his mouth.
